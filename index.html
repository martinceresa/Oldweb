Welcome to my Web Page

PhD student in Computer Science at CIFASIS. Advised by Mauro Jaskelioff. My research focuses on Improvement Theory in a Lazy Functional Environment. Improvement Theory has been developed extensively by Prof. Sands early in the nineties. It is based on the notion of Improvement presented as a refinement of Equality of programs. So starting from a notion of equality my idea is to derive a notion of improvement. Having defined such a notion, there is an entire theory to develop and that is my goal right now. In particular, my efforts are in present an Improvement Theory with effectful computations.

Before coming to CIFASIS, I was a student in the department of Computer Science at Universidad Nacional de Rosario from where I graduated in March, 2015.

In 2013, I worked as intern at IMDEA (Madrid, Spain) under the tutelage of Benedikt Schmidt, in the development of automatic tactics in EasyCrypt. In this internship I have learned about Modern Cryptography Proofs, and a bit about tactic development. I developed an automatic tactic (or megatactic) which implemented a classic cryptography step called optimistic sampling.

My interests include: theoretical computer science, programming languages theory, type theory, category theory, mathematics, foundation of mathematics, topology and logic. Recently I have found my self studying history of mathematics and philosophy of mathematics.

I like to solve problems in ProjectEuler.

But right now I am more focused on Haskell/Agda problems on Codewars.

In my free time moments, I like to meditate (mindfulness), do some Yoga (Hatha/Dynamic) or even better practice Capoeira! I love to hangout with my friends and enjoy myself reading to death.
